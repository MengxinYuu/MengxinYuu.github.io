/ THE CHALLENGE

FOUR REQUIREMENTS

Break Through The Traditional Assessment

Originally, taking an online assessment is simple: you answer the questions and you get a score. However, with the rapid development of Computerized Adaptive Testing and Personalized Assessment Services, our stakeholders had more expectations on what a good assessment could do:

1) Analyze and Visualize students' learning competencies rather than test scores (Students with same scores may have different learning competencies).
2) Create students’ profiles and Track long-term learning (Students could compare with themselves).
3) Compare students’ learning competencies across time and region (Students could compare with others).
4) Provide follow-up learning strategies (Students could know how to be improved).

The new requirements demanded highly on the data, which presented two new challenges.

TWO CHALLENGES

Ensure The Consistency and Compatibility

1) Data Consistency : The data flow before / during / after an assessment needs to be consistent to ensure the efficiency of the data analysis. Therefore, the assessment process needs to be fully covered by our product to ensure data consistency.

2) System Compatibility : As the R&D Center we support multiple branches in TAL. Therefore, what we designed for Xueersi and Izhikang needs to be easily integrated into other business lines.

ONE GOAL

Build An Internal Assessment Ecosystem in One Year

With the first step collaborated with Izhikang and Xueersi, our final ambition is to create a strong foundation that could embrace various business lines and diverse user base. Therefore, we decided to build —— not only an assessment app or data analysis tool —— but a whole ecosystem that could cover the entire assessment process with a full-set package of assessment tools.

/ THE SOLUTION

UX TIMELINE

Do What's Right, The Right Way, at The Right Time

We had limited time for UX research & design, even though they are necessary for product decision-making. Therefore, we separated our timeline into two phases: One for building the MVP, where we spent 6 months doing the large-scale user researches, visiting schools across 7 cities in China, researching multiple roles including instructors, students, parents, and schools' directors. One for making agile iterations, where we have no time for large-scale user research in a 2-week scrum, but instead we make full use of user data and user feedback collected from the released products to make better design iterations.

INTRODUCING TAL ASSESSMENT ECOSYSTEM

Design for All Types of Assessments

Through the large-scale user researches conducted in the early stage, we found out that even though the scenarios of an assessment are different —— some are taken online and some are taken at school —— the key process is kind of similar. To ensure that our ecosystem could be easily integrated into various business lines with different types of assessment, we extracted the most necessary user actions from 10+ assessment scenarios, and chuncked these actions into three parts with three design goals:

  • Before the assessment, we need a system that could support instructors to build assessment with high quality.
  • During the assessment, we need an app where student's interactions and response data could fully record.
  • After the assessment, we need a data platform to analyze / visualize data, and provide further learning / teaching strategies smartly.

/ BEFORE THE ASSESSMENT

INTRODUCING PAC ASSESSMENT SYSTEM

Provide Instructors with One-stop Service for Developing Good Assessments

The key to building a good assessment lies in preparing the right questions — normally a heavy burden on instructors. To ensure the quality of the assessment creation and to reduce the instructors' workload, PAC System is designed with a full set of functions & tools that every instructor could benefit from and feels easy to use.

Tag Assessment Questions Easily And Smartly

The accuracy of the question tagging determines the quality of the assessment and data. PAC System maintains a database where 50,000+ assessment questions have been carefully tagged and ready to use. But if instructors want to use new questions without tags, PAC System will support them to speed up the work through user-friendly interfaces and simple workflows.

Build All Types of Assessments in One Click

Different assessments need to be built for different purposes, which worries instructors a lot: Where to find a placement test, or an in-class quiz? How to build an assessment that is a little bit challenging for the average, or an assessment focused mainly on selected knowledge points?
PAC System allows instructors to choose the assessment templates, knowledge components, level of difficulty, question types... and to build whatever they need with the automated questions generation.

Customize The Assessment to Your Needs

Wanna make it more personalized? No worries. PAC System provides plenty of options for customization, such as changing the interfaces or designing personalized feedback. With a series of templates and toolkits, instructors are encouraged to create assessments 100% tailored to their needs.

SUCCESS METRICS & ITERATION

Good Functions, Good Experiences

Every two weeks, we conduct Heuristic Evaluation based on the Five Elements of UX (by Jesse James Garret). We analyze UX elements including the user flow / the information architecture / the interaction methods / the UI design / ... and put what we find to scrum backlog for futural iterations.

/ DURING THE ASSESSMENT

INTRODUCING ZHIKANG ASSESSMENT APP

Provide Students with Engaging Experiences of Assessment

The traditional assessment process is an one-way information transfer — students are given the paper, then answer the questions, and finally receive the scores. No interactions, no communication, and no in-assessment learning occurred in between.

Zhikang Assessment App is designed for interactive testing and learning from testing. With plenty of in-test functions including adaptive testing, immediate feedback, in-test data collection and interactive score report, students will be more engaged during the assessment.

Empower Students with Assessment Preview & Paper Analysis

Students will never benefit from a test if they only see the question but have no idea what it is testing for. During an assessment, students always ask themselves: What knowledge component tested by this question? Is it a hard question or an easy one? I’m stuck on this question, but why? Is it because I'm not good at this question itself, or the question is too difficult, or I need to work harder on this question type?
Zhikang Assessment provides a well-designed in-test assessment preview and paper analysis, ensuring students to see the whole picture of an assessment, including:

1) The question type.
2) The tested knowledge components (KC).
3) The difficulty distribution and difficulty analysis.
4) The average response time.

Motivate Students with Immediate Feedback & Gamification (Conceptual Feature)

1) Provide immediate feedback in test: not only “assessment of learning”, but also “assessment for learning”.
2) Record students in-test journaling: not only their answers, but also how they got there and their attempts.
3) Motivate students with gamification and engaging assessment experiences.

Engage Students with In-Test Journaling & Interactive Test Report

Evidence of learning does not exist in the final test score, but hides in any attempts, choices, or struggles made in the assessment. Learning is a process, but most students are given the result in the assessment.

Zhikang Assessment quantifies students' thinking by engaging students with in-test journaling, and visualizes students' learning by providing interactive test report. By interacting with the report (e.g., clicking the chart, comparing response time, dragging and dropping the knowledge component nodes, etc), students will be motivated to click around and do the test analysis on their own, and will take more ownership over their learning.

DESIGN PROCESS OF ZHIKANG ASSESSMENT APP

How We Got There Step by Step

I'll use the design process of our interactive test report as an example to demonstrate how we got there step by step.

Step One: Decide What to Show & How to Show

The process that we decided on what & how to visualize can be separated into three steps.
1) Normally, learning scientists decided the big picture of what to show for the report. The first step we took was to fully understand their data requirements and ensured we were on the same page.
2) Neither do students know the terminologies in academic, nor do their care about them. To make the visualization student-centered and student-friendly, it's important to speak their language. For instance, when visualizing the Knowledge Component Tree put forward by learning scientists, we translated the Level Three Knowledge Components in academic to what students feel familiar with —— the chapter.
3) An exciting design in mind may be a disaster for developers to build. In the early stage, any design ideas would be presented to developers to achieve a balance between design and development.
4) Considering what learning scientists want to show, what students hope to know, and what developers are capable to build, we decided on the visualization and built mock-ups ready for the user test.
