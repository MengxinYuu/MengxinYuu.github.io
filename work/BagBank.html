WHO AM I DESIGNING FOR

Say Hello to Morden Football Fans

My design focused on a younger generation - the morden football fans aging under 30. Most morden football fans are priced out of the live matches. However, the proliferation of videos, news and social platforms provides them with a more unfettered access to the matches than any generation before. The paradoxical environment they're facing with makes them the most promising user group of our mobile app.

The Participatory-Focused Fan

Participatory-focused fans enjoy the feeling of experiencing something together. For them, the mere psychological act of connecting and belonging is an extremely strong motivation. One unique behavior of them is "two-screening" the match , which means using one device to watch the game while using another one to chat on social media. Therefore, the biggest pinpoint for them is switching from different devices interrupts their experience when watching the game.

The Spectator-Focused Fan

Spectator-focused fans not only watch the lives but also enjoy reading related news, interviews, reports and comments. They are proud of being a "know-it-all" of the team and highly focus on stats. Before the game, they like to make their predictions of the scores based on stats they got. After the game, they enjoy searching for stats and reviews from authoritative media. Their biggest pinpoint is, the football stats they need are sometimes hard to find and read.

The Occasional Visitor

The occasional visitors are not actually fans so I considered them as the secondary users. They are occasional fans that are trying to relate to someone in their life, such as a girl dating with a fan man, a daughter in a fan family, or a person surrounded by a banch of fan friends. Since occasional fans don't "live and die" with their team, they watch football only when they can find time. Their problem is, they may have no time or motivation to watch the whole game so they focus more on the highlight part.
